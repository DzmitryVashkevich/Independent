EVEREST: THE HIGHEST MOUNTAIN IN THE WORLD

EVEREST: THE HIGHEST MOUNTAIN IN THE WORLD Height: 8848 m. The list of the highest mountains in the world is headed by Mount Everest (Chomolungma). It is also included in the list of seven peaks - the highest peaks of all parts of the world. True, in 2019, French scientists confirmed that it is not Everest that should be considered the highest point, but the extinct Chimborazo volcano in Ecuador. They recalled that the radius of the Earth at the equator is almost 20 km wider than at the poles, and if you measure the height from the center of the planet, Chimborazo is higher than Everest - 6384 m against 6381 m. Scientists confirmed their assumption empirically. However, this statement is still controversial, since the height of the mountains can change over time. So, after the earthquake in Nepal in 2015, Chinese surveyors monitored the state of the highest mountain. As it turned out, from 2005 to 2015, Everest moved to the northeast at a speed of 4 cm per year and each year “grew” by 0.3 cm. In ten years, the mountain moved 40 cm and became 3 cm higher. But .. . after the earthquake decreased by 2.54 cm. So far, the official height of Everest is 8848 m, but experts from Nepal and China decided to double-check it. The results should be announced by the end of 2020.


CHOGORI MOUNTAIN KILLER

CHOGORI MOUNTAIN KILLER Height: 8611 m. Chogori is the second highest mountain in the world and the only one of the ten highest mountains located in the Karakorum. The rest lie in the Himalayas. It is also known to scientists as K-2, where the letter "K" means "Karakoram", and 2 is the serial number of the peak, which was awarded to it by British Colonel Thomas Montgomery in 1856. The people also call it the killer mountain, since few manage to climb to the top. By 2010, Everest had been conquered 5104 times, and K-2 - 302. There were also women among the climbers. So, the first to climb the peak was the Pole Wanda Rutkevich in 1986. And in 2011, the Austrian climber Gerlinde Kaltenbrunner conquered not only Chogori, but also other highest peaks of the planet, and the first one did it without an oxygen tank.
